Excludable	person can be prevented from using a good
Rival	one person's use of a good diminishes others' use
Private Good	Rival and Excludable
Club Good	Non-Rival and Excludable
Common Resource	Rival and Non-Excludable
Public Good	Non-Rival and Non-Excludable
Free Rider Problem	Happens to non-excludable goods, people can consume a good without paying for it, therefore the good is not produced, fixed with taxes (happens with common resources and public goods)
Tragedy of the Commons	Common goods will eventually run out because people cannot be stopped from consuming them, but they are rival, fixed with gov. intervention (happens with common resources)
Explicit Costs	Require an outlay of money
Implicit Costs	Do not require an outlay of money (ex. opportunity cost of time)
Accounting Profit	TR-explicit costs
Economic Profit	TR-TC (explicit and implicit costs)
Marginal Product of Labor	ΔQ/ΔL
Diminishing Marginal Product of Labor	Marginal product of an input declines as the quantity of input increases (ex. bc the next worker is not as productive)
Marginal Cost (MC)	ΔTC/ΔQ
Average Fixed Cost (AFC)	FC/Q
Average Variable Cost (AVC)	VC/Q
Average Total Cost (ATC)	TC/Q OR AVC+AFC
Efficient Production in the Short Run	MC=ATC (ATC is minimized where it crosses with MC)
Perfect Competition	price takers, many buyers and sellers, homogenous goods, little to no barriers to entry/exit, MR=P only here because you can change the output without changing the price
Total Revenue (TR)	P*Q
Average Revenue (AR)	TR/Q = P
Marginal Revenue (MR)	ΔTR/ΔQ
Profit if Producing (PC)	(P-ATC)*Q
Profit if Not Producing (PC)	-FC
How much do you produce in Perfect Competition?	Find q where P(MR)=MC
Whether or not to produce in PC? (Short Run)	YES when P>AVC
NO when P<AVC
Whether or not to produce in PC? (Long Run)	YES when P>ATC
NO when P<ATC
Monopoly	One firm that is the sole producer of a good that does not have close substitutes, price maker because they have market power, high barriers to entry
Market Power	ability to influence market price
How much to produce? (Monopoly)	Find q where MC=MR then go up to D curve
Whether to produce? (Monopoly)	Short Run: YES when P>AVC, NO when P<AVC
Long Run: YES when P>ATC, NO when P<ATV
Price Discrimination	selling the same good at different prices to different buyers
Perfect Price Discrimination	Charging each buyer his or her willingness to pay (WTP)
Oligopoly	market structure where in which only a few sellers offer similar or identical products
Concentration Ratio	percentage of the market's total output supplied by its 4 largest firms
Game Theory	study of how people behave in strategic situations
Collusion	An agreement among firms in a market about quantities to produce or prices to charge
Cartel	a group of firms acting in unison
Nash Equilibrium	a situation in which economic participants interacting with one another each choose their best strategy given the strategies that all others have chosen
Dominant Strategy	Strategy that is best for a player in a game regardless of strategies chosen by other players
Prisoners' Dilemma	game between 2 captured criminals that illustrates why cooperation is difficult even when it is mutually beneficial
Tit-for-Tat	Whatever your rival does in one round, you do in the following round
Budget Constraint	The limit on the consumption bundles that a consumer can afford
Indifference Curve	Shows consumption bundles that give the consumer the same level of satisfaction
Marginal Rate of Substitution	Rate at which a consumer is willing to trade one good - slope of indifference curve - opportunity cost of 1 of the goods on the X axis
Normal Good	increase in income increases quantity demanded
Inferior Good	reduction in income reduces quantity demanded
Output Effect	Higher output raises revenue
Price Effect	Lower prices reduce revenue
